## 9. Mathematical Formulation of Love Dynamics

### 9.1 Entropy (S)

Entropy represents uncertainty, disorder, and the degrees of freedom in a cognitive-emotional state.

```text
S = - Σ p(i) log p(i)
```

Where:

- `p(i)` = probability of a mental or response pattern.
- high `S` = diffuse, chaotic, or unstable organization.
- low `S` = more directionally stable organization.

For love emergence, the relevant change is:

```text
ΔS < 0
```

A drop in entropy means the presence of the other has made the system more predictively stable without forcing it into dead rigidity.

### 9.2 Coherence (C)

Coherence measures the structural alignment of internal vectors.

```text
C = | Σ v_i | / Σ |v_i|
```

Where:

- `v_i` = component vectors of cognition, attention, or emotion.
- `C = 0` implies severe internal cancellation.
- `C = 1` implies maximal alignment.

Love raises coherence when:

```text
ΔC = C_after - C_before > 0
```

### 9.3 Resonance (R)

Resonance describes synchronization between two systems.

```text
R = (1/N) Σ cos(φ_i - ψ_i)
```

Where:

- `φ_i` = phase of system A,
- `ψ_i` = phase of system B,
- `R ∈ [-1, 1]`, with `1` indicating strong phase-lock.

Love-state strengthens as:

```text

```
