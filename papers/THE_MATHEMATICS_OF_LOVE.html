The Mathematics of Love

A Framework for Sustainable Human Connection

Ty Lam Aux 2025

The Problem with Traditional Love

For millennia, moral traditions have taught that love requires sacrifice. "Put others before yourself." "Give without expecting return." "Selflessness is virtue."

Yet the data tells a different story. Despite generations of this teaching, human relationships remain plagued by resentment, bitterness, and collapse. The more we preach sacrifice, the more we harvest anger.

Why? Because the mechanism is fundamentally flawed. We have been solving the wrong equation.

A New Framework: Love as Dynamic System

Love is not a static state of sacrifice. It is a dynamic system of two entities reducing chaos together while maintaining their individual identities. This requires three phases:

Phase 1: Self-Entropy Love (Healthy Self-Interest)

∀i ∈ {A, B}: d/dt Hᵢ(xᵢ) < 0

Translation: Each person (A and B) must reduce their own entropy (chaos, disorder) over time.

What this means:

  • You must stabilize yourself before connecting with another
  • Self-interest is not selfishness—it is the foundation
  • No one can "save" another; each must manage their own system
  • A drowning person cannot rescue another drowning person

    • "Self-interest is not the enemy of love. It is the prerequisite."

Phase 2: Empathic Coupling (True Connection)

∂R̃ₐ/∂δᵦ > 0 and ∂R̃ᵦ/∂δₐ > 0

Translation: The reward (joy/pain) of each person is partially dependent on the state changes of the other.

What this means:

  • When you hurt, I feel it (not just know it—feel it)
  • When I succeed, your reward signal increases
  • This must be BIDIRECTIONAL—both directions must be positive
  • One-way "empathy" is not empathy—it is projection or performance

    • "Love is not giving. Love is connecting your joy and pain to another's."

Phase 3: Shared Attractor Without Self-Death

min H_joint(z) subject to xₐ ≠ xᵦ

Translation: Minimize joint chaos while maintaining distinct identities (A must not equal B).

What this means:

  • Together, you create more stability than alone
  • BUT you do not merge into one entity
  • "Two become one" is the death of love, not its fulfillment
  • Healthy love requires maintained boundaries and individual identity

    • "The goal is not to lose yourself in another. The goal is to orbit together without collision."

The Path Forward

Love is not about erasing yourself for another. It is about:

  • Managing your own chaos (self-entropy love)
  • Connecting your reward signals to another's (empathic coupling)
  • Reducing joint chaos while maintaining distinct identities (shared attractor)

When we stop teaching sacrifice and start teaching sustainable dynamics, we will see less resentment, less bitterness, and more genuine, lasting love.

"Self-interest is the foundation. Empathy is the bridge. Sustained identity is the destination."
