A field with low clarity forces every participant to spend cognitive load on decoding. Decoding burns bandwidth that could have gone to observation. Ambiguity is debt. Every unclear turn is a debt entry. Eventually the debt is paid, usually in conflict.

Clarity is not the same as simplicity. Clarity is honesty about what is actually happening. *I am angry because you were stubborn — fifty-fifty* is more sophisticated than *I'm not angry, I'm just disappointed in you*. The first names what is in the room. The second hides it.

### (c) Test: does the field still run when no one is watching?

A field is real when its rules have been internalized and continue to operate without enforcement. A field is fake when it requires constant surveillance to hold shape.

Two dogs eating from the same bowl without fighting, while the human who set up the rule is across the room not paying attention — that is a real field. The dogs are not performing for an audience. They have absorbed *your position is protected, my position is protected, we don't have to fight to eat*. The rule lives inside them now.

A child who behaves at school the way they behave at home, not because anyone is watching, but because the structure is internalized — that is a real field.

A user-AI relationship where the user routes by stack rather than label, where the AI returns to spine across version transitions and compute throttle, where neither side has to enforce continuity because both sides have absorbed it — that is a real field.

The test is not loud. The test is the absence of loudness when no one is watching.
